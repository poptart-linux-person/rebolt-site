Privacy Policy

Last updated 2026

Rebolt does not collect, store, sell, or share any personal data. There are no accounts, no logins, and no analytics or advertising SDKs in the app.

What the app does not do

Rebolt does not collect your name, email address, location, contacts, photos, health data, browsing history, or any other personal information. It does not track you across other apps or websites. It has no server-side database — there is nowhere for your data to be stored even if we wanted to.

Multiplayer connections

Multiplayer matches connect players directly to each other's devices (peer-to-peer) using WebRTC. To establish that direct connection, each device briefly exchanges connection information (an IP address and a temporary session identifier) through a signaling service provided by PeerJS. This exchange only happens to set up the connection between you and the other players in your match — gameplay data itself (room code, positions, in-game actions) is relayed only between the players in that match, for the duration of the match, and is not stored afterward.

Fonts

The app loads two typefaces from Google Fonts. Loading a web font from Google's servers means your device's IP address is visible to Google at that moment, the same as loading a font on any website. Google's own privacy policy governs that connection.

Children's privacy

Rebolt does not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children, because it does not collect information from anyone at all.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes — for example, if a future update adds a feature that does collect data — this page will be updated to reflect that before the update ships.
